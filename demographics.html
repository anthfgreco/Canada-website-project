About four-fifths of the population lives within 150 kilometres. In 2006, the average age was 39.5 years, and by 2011 it was 39.9 years. As of 2013, the average life expectancy for Canadians is 81 years. Canada ranks first in the number of adults having post-secondary education, with 51 percent of Canadian adults attaining at least an undergraduate college or university degree. The literacy rate also contributes to this factor at an astounding 99 percent literacy rate.

Population

The 2011 Canadian census counted a total population of 33,476,688 people, an increase of about 6 percent from the 2006 census. Between 1990 and 2008, the population increased by 5.6 million, equivalent to a 20 percent increase in growth. The main causes for this immense growth is immigration. Canada is experiencing a shift towards an older population, with more people retiring and less of a working age group.
