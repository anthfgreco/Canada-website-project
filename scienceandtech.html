Scientific Achievements

In 2012 alone, Canada spent approximately $31.3 billion on scientific research and development. As of 2015, Canada has produced thirteen Nobel Prizes in various sectors such as physics, chemistry, and medicine. It is also ranked fourth worldwide for scientific research quality in a 2012 scientific survey.

Inventions and Innovations

Science and Medicine

  • Medicinal insulin by Frederick Banting, Charles Best, and James Collip (1922)
  • First practical electron microscope by James Hillier and Arthur Prebus (1939)
  • A process to extract the Bromine element by Herbert Henry Dow (1890)

Food and Agriculture

  • Canola (1970)
  • Marquis Wheat by Charles E. Saunders (~1900)
  • McIntosh Red apple by John McIntosh
  • Early form of peanut butter by Marcellus Gilmore Edson (1884)
  • Poutine

Communication

  • The Walkie-Talkie by Donald L. Hings for military use (1942)
  • Standard time by Sir Sandford Fleming (1878)
  • The pager by Alfred J. Gross (1949)
  • The 56k modem by Dr. Brent Townshend in 1996

Transportation and Mobility

  • First commerical jetliner flown in North America by James C. Floyd (1949)
  • The Canadarm
  • The snowmobile by Joseph-Armand Bombardier (1937)
  • Prosthetic hand by Helmut Lucas (1971)

Defence and Military

  • First widely used military gas mask by Cluny MacPherson (1915)
  • The G-suit by Wilbur R. Franks (1941)
  • Military Sonar by Reginald Fessenden

Sports and Entertainment

  • Lacrosse by William George Beers (~1860)
  • Basketball by James Naismith (1891)
  • Ice Hockey (~1900)
  • Table Hockey by Donald Munro (~1930)
  • The goalie mask by Jacques Plante (1959)
  • Five pin bowling by Thomas F. Ryan (1955)
